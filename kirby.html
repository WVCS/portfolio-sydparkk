Kirby

By: Sydney Park


In this website, I will proceed to talk about Kirby, the best (and cutest) Super Smash Bros character of all time.

Of course, there are many other characters in SSB that are fundamentally better, such as:

Ness and Lucas,



Ridley,



and Chrom.

However, Kirby has been a popular character throughout his time and here's why:

Kirby is a game character created by Nintendo in 1992. His lore is very straight forward: evil villain comes and tries to destroy his planet and then he goes to fight them, ultimately winning.
Do that aout 9 more times throughout all of his games and there you go. Through this lore, he is the obvious hero, and, as cliche as it sounds, he's that good.

Over the years of development, Kirby's appearance has changed drastically since he was first released in 1993.

Year Appearance
1992 "Kirby's Dream Land"

1996 "Kirby Super Star"

2000 "Kirby 64: The Crystal Shards"

2006 "Kirby: Squeak Squad"

2011 "Kirby's Return to Dream Land"

As you can see, he looks fire throughout his evolution. He has always been a cute ball of pink.

I will now explain why he's (sadly but truly) my main in SSB.



His powers are extraordinary if you learn to use them well.
His hammer makes a ton of damage on the opponent, especially once it has been heated up and is ready for swing;



he turns to stone, which helps prevent anybody from harming him and although it may seem counterproductive
because he cannot move himself, it is still one of the only ways a charachter has a reliable self-defense mechanism;



his power suck can inhale a different character and turn him into that character, therefore Kirby has access to every characters' powers, giving him more options;



and his ultimate power is flawless as long as you aim correctly.


Overall, he's the best character to play with and the easiest to understand. Ever since I first started playing this on the PS3 in 1st grade,
I've always played Kirby and now with our own Switch, I experiment with other characters, but of course, I always go back to Kirby.

That concludes my presentation on Kirby.
